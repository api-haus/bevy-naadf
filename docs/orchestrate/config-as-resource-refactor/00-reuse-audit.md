## Current AppArgs shape (tallies)

- **`AppArgs` field count: 16** (`grid_preset`, `taa`, `taa_ring_depth`, `gi`, `construction_config`, `spawn_test_entity`, `resize_test`, `vox_e2e_mode`, `oasis_edit_visual_mode`, `small_edit_visual_mode`, `small_edit_repro_mode`, `vox_gpu_construction_mode`, `vox_gpu_oracle_cpu_phase`, `vox_gpu_oracle_gpu_phase`, `vox_web_parity_skybox_phase`, `vox_web_parity_loaded_phase`, `vox_horizon_native_phase`) — actually 17 if you count the embedded `gi` and `construction_config` substructs as one each. (16 distinct top-level fields after collapsing the two structs to one slot each.) Source: `app_args.rs:25-183`.
- **`Res<AppArgs>` / `Res<crate::AppArgs>` read sites: 14** distinct `system parameter` declarations across 14 files (one per file, except `render/construction/mod.rs` which has 2, and `render/extract.rs` which has 2 `Extract<Option<Res<crate::AppArgs>>>` extracts).
- **`args.<field>` accesses: ~169** raw matches in `rg`, but ~24 of those are in `bin/e2e_render.rs` referring to its LOCAL `argv: Vec<String>` (named `args`), not `AppArgs`. True `AppArgs.<field>` access count ≈ **130-140** sites across ~20 files. Includes ~50 read-only access patterns inside e2e gate `run_*` builders that exist only because the e2e-mode booleans are routed through `AppArgs` instead of a dedicated `E2eGateMode` resource (see Borderline).
- **`ResMut<AppArgs>` sites: 3** — `settings::adjust_settings` (`settings/mod.rs:458`), `settings::mouse_interact_settings` (`settings/mod.rs:528`), and `voxel::web_vox::startup_fetch_default_vox` (`voxel/web_vox.rs:390`). Plus the local `let mut app_args = AppArgs::default()` constructions in the 14 bootstrap call sites.
- **`AppArgs::default()` direct construction: 17 sites** across `lib.rs` (1, in `build_app`), `main.rs` (1), `android_main.rs` (1, via `build_app_with_budget`), `bin/e2e_render.rs` (2), 10 e2e gate `run_*` builders, and 1 in `settings::mod.rs` (test only).

## Borderline calls

- **`AppArgs` is runtime-mutated by the Settings panel.** `settings::adjust_settings` and `settings::mouse_interact_settings` take `ResMut<AppArgs>` and mutate `args.gi` fields in response to keyboard/mouse input. Definition at `settings/mod.rs:458, 528`. This is NOT bootstrap-only — `args.gi` is a live editable resource. Implication: the refactor cannot model `args.gi` as a transient bootstrap-only struct; the GI knobs are a long-lived runtime resource that happens to be SEEDED at bootstrap. Suggested resolution: lift `GiSettings` (already a standalone struct in `settings/canonical.rs`) into its own `Resource`, the settings panel takes `ResMut<GiSettings>`, the extract reads `Res<GiSettings>`.
- **`AppArgs.grid_preset` is mutated at Startup time by `web_vox::startup_fetch_default_vox`.** `voxel/web_vox.rs:390-405` does `args.grid_preset = GridPreset::WebSkybox`. This is the only OTHER `ResMut<AppArgs>` outside settings. Implication: even `grid_preset` (which sounds bootstrap-only) is mutated post-bootstrap. The `?skybox=1` resolution must happen BEFORE `setup_test_grid` reads it (enforced today by `.before(...)` ordering — `lib.rs:838-842` per the docstring, though that exact line is on a different layout in this worktree). The refactor needs to preserve this Startup-ordering constraint OR move the `?skybox=1` resolution earlier into bootstrap proper.
- **The 11 e2e-mode boolean fields on `AppArgs` are conceptually a separate domain.** `resize_test`, `vox_e2e_mode`, `oasis_edit_visual_mode`, `small_edit_visual_mode`, `small_edit_repro_mode`, `vox_gpu_construction_mode`, `vox_gpu_oracle_cpu_phase`, `vox_gpu_oracle_gpu_phase`, `vox_web_parity_skybox_phase`, `vox_web_parity_loaded_phase`, `vox_horizon_native_phase` (per `app_args.rs:65-182`). The comment at `app_args.rs:7-9` already flags "at most one e2e flag is true at a time — the enum collapse is deferred to a future Step 8 (D6+D7 paired) since it crosses 11 mode files." Implication: this collapse is in-scope for the refactor — these are NOT 11 independent domains; they are one `E2eGateMode` enum. The `WindowConfig::window_for_e2e_args` dispatcher at `window_config.rs:153-163` already treats them as mutually exclusive.
- **`AppArgs.taa_ring_depth` is the field the user named.** The smell shape: `taa_ring_depth` exists ONLY because `TaaRingConfig` (which IS its own resource — `render/taa.rs:46-50`) is plumbed via a plugin-build snapshot rather than an extract. The budget routine at `lib.rs:156-167` MUTATES `args.taa_ring_depth` BEFORE `build_app_with_args`, which is the workaround for the snapshot timing. A clean refactor would remove `taa_ring_depth` from `AppArgs` entirely: `TaaRingConfig` becomes the main-world resource (default `DEFAULT_TAA_RING_DEPTH`), the budget inserts an override, and the render world reads via extract (matching `EffectiveWorldSize`).
- **`AppArgs.construction_config` is borderline already-extracted.** It's an embedded `ConstructionConfig` struct, lifted via `From<&AppArgs>` into a standalone render-world `ConstructionConfig` resource. The `AppArgs` field is consumed at exactly two sites: the `From<&AppArgs>` lift in `render/construction/config.rs:265-288` (which on wasm32 ALSO mutates `max_group_bound_dispatch` + `n_bounds_rounds` — a platform-divergence injection that needs a new home), and `run_gpu_construction_startup` at `render/construction/mod.rs:1787-1804` (which reads `construction_config.gpu_construction_enabled`). The e2e gate builders also mutate it directly (`vox_gpu_construction.rs:234`, etc). Implication: this is the easiest field to remove from `AppArgs` — the resource already exists; the refactor is "move the `From<&AppArgs>` lift to a bootstrap path that just inserts the resource directly, then delete the field."
- **`AppArgs.spawn_test_entity` is dual-purpose.** Used as a `Startup` system gate (`render/construction/mod.rs:1853`) AND as a runtime flag read by the e2e driver (`e2e/driver.rs:680`). The Startup gate could be reframed as "if the Phase-C test-entity resource is present, spawn it" instead.
- **`AppArgs` carries no test-only fields, no `#[cfg(...)]`-gated fields.** Clean on that axis — no platform-specific divergence inside the struct itself (the wasm32-only mutation lives in `ConstructionConfig::From<&AppArgs>`, not on the struct).
- **Diagnostics dump reads `args.grid_preset / taa / taa_ring_depth / spawn_test_entity / gi / construction_config`.** `diagnostics.rs:104-123`. Cross-cutting consumer — needs to know about all 6 of those fields. Resolution: after refactor, diagnostics takes `Option<Res<GridPreset>>`, `Option<Res<TaaConfig>>`, etc. — six independent reads instead of one bag. Cleaner per the principle but more system parameters.
- **Settings panel readonly knobs.** `KnobKind::Readonly { value: fn(&AppArgs) -> String }` at `settings/mod.rs:138-140`. After refactor, the `fn(&AppArgs) -> String` signature changes — needs to take whatever the new per-domain resources are, or fan out to multiple functions.
- **Test-only `#[cfg(test)] mod tests` in `app_args.rs:209-236`.** Asserts `AppArgs::default().taa_ring_depth == 32`. After removing the field, this assertion moves to wherever `TaaRingConfig::default()` lives.
- **`render::construction::config::ConstructionConfig::From<&AppArgs>` clamps `max_group_bound_dispatch` + `n_bounds_rounds` on wasm32** (`render/construction/config.rs:265-288`). When the `AppArgs` field goes away, this platform-divergence injection needs a new home — likely the bootstrap-side resource insertion point on wasm32, or a `fn ConstructionConfig::for_target_arch() -> Self` constructor.
- **The `From<&AppArgs>` lift pattern is already in use ONLY for `ConstructionConfig`.** No other field has its own `From<&AppArgs>` impl. The refactor's per-domain resources should NOT generically adopt `From<&AppArgs>` as the bootstrap path; the cleaner direction is for bootstrap to construct each resource directly from a transient `BootstrapInputs` struct that itself was built from CLI/argv + budget probe.

## Side notes / observations / complaints

- **The principle is already 1/3 implemented.** Three of the existing per-domain resources (`AppConfig`, `EffectiveWorldSize`, `InvalidSampleStorageCount`) NEVER pass through `AppArgs`. Two more (`TaaRingConfig`, `ConstructionConfig`) DO pass through `AppArgs` but only as a "snapshot at plugin-build time" relay — the relay is the smell. The refactor is mostly "delete the relay" rather than "invent new resources."
- **The e2e gate-mode boolean explosion is the largest single migration.** 11 mutually-exclusive bool fields in `AppArgs`, each consulted exactly twice (once when a gate's `run_*` flips it on, once when the e2e driver branches on it). The architect should weigh: (a) collapse to a single `E2eGateMode: GateKind` enum resource (clean; needs to land alongside the `e2e_render.rs` GateKind dispatch refactor that's already half-done at `bin/e2e_render.rs:111-122`), or (b) one resource per gate (literal, faithful, but 11 new types).
- **There is no `BootstrapInputs` type today, only the implicit "the local `AppArgs` variable in `main.rs` / `android_main.rs` / each e2e `run_*`".** A clean refactor likely introduces such a type as the transient parser output (the analogue of `BudgetCaps` for non-budget config) and then has bootstrap fan it out into the per-domain resources. This would be a NEW type, not a reuse candidate, but its shape can mirror `BudgetCaps`.
- **`build_app_with_budget` already does the right thing.** It runs the probe, decomposes the result into per-domain resources (`EffectiveWorldSize`, `InvalidSampleStorageCount`, and writes back into `args.taa_ring_depth` because `TaaRingConfig` doesn't have its own override path yet). The refactor extends this pattern to the rest of `AppArgs`.
- **The `web_vox::startup_fetch_default_vox` Startup-time mutation of `args.grid_preset`** is a structural smell independent of the refactor — Startup systems mutating bootstrap state is exactly the pattern the user is critiquing. But fixing it requires moving the `?skybox=1` URL-param resolution out of `Startup` and into the wasm32 bootstrap in `main.rs:73-90`. Worth flagging to the architect: the cleanup is in scope for the refactor but requires touching the wasm bootstrap.
- **Vigilance note.** I verified every cited file:line with `Read` (or `rg -n` against the exact line shown). Line numbers are accurate for this worktree (`feat/android-build`) as of read time; the `app_args.rs:65-182` range covers the 11 e2e-mode booleans (verified by `wc -l app_args.rs` = 236).
- **One brief over-constraint.** The brief asks for "approximate count of fields" + "approximate count of read sites" but the precise counts came out clean and useful — left them precise. The "approximate" framing was meant to scope-protect against the architect's full inventory, which is fine; the precise counts here are sized for scoping, not field-by-field.
- **The brief's split between "audit precedents" (this dispatch) and "field-by-field inventory" (architect's dispatch) is good.** I did NOT enumerate each `AppArgs` field's consumer sites — that's the architect's downstream work. I confirmed the inventory is bounded (16 fields, ~14 reader systems, ~130-140 `args.<field>` access sites) so the architect can budget for it.
